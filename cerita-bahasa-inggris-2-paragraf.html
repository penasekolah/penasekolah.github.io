'Of course, but we can't spend it spree like before. It's our saving.'

'What?!? you're joking, right? what expenses should I cut? That's my normal expenses. Now how we're supposed to live day by day if we don't use the saving? Honey, you can't be serious. 'Really? can't you just have some sympathy for me? At the time like this, people try to live with all they have carefully. Everybody's suffering due to this outbreak. 'Excuse me? I am not them. This is my life, whether you like it or not, it's your job as a husband to fulfill my needs.'

'But where I can get a job at the time like this? It's simply ...'

'I don't care, screw corona, it's just tiny little thing.'

'OH MY GOD, you are being absurd now, hon. Can't you even have a heart to really see what's going on in the whole world now? It might be tiny but it's deadly.'

'Deadly? Nothing is as deadly as poverty. Now don't you dare coming back until you get a job. Surely an ex data analyst in Virgin Airlines like you can easily get a new one. GO NOW!!'

'Unbelievable, fine, if that's what you want.'

He wandered around with his mobile phone on his hand to find any possible vacancy he could apply.  He was so upset about what his wife said that he couldn't care less to wear a facemask. He decided to sleep there at the couch at the lobby on the second floor.

' Where is he? Why don't he answer the phone? Could he be seeing someone else? How dare he?', said Lucille to herself.

But she kept ringing and ringing. After a while, she decided to go to Kodjo's office.

When she got there, she saw the security guard at the gate and asked him if her husband was here. He only said nobody went inside after 2 pm. However, suddenly, he heard a screaming noise from upstairs. It was the cleaning lady. She went straight downstairs and whispered the security to check on what she saw. Thus, they ran upstairs after the guard told Lucille to stay at the security office.

'Oh no, this is bad. Bad.', said the security guard while finding the unmoved pale body on the couch.

'So, is it really, Mr.Akale?', asked the cleaning lady to confirm.
